Vision : To ensure Nutrition Security for all individuals with difficulty in chewing and swallowing, providing access to safe, nutritious food that can be consumed bio-synchronously to meets their dietary needs and food preferences for a healthy life. We aim to create a Dysphagia-Free India where timely, evidence-based care prevents complications and improves quality of life. Through multidisciplinary strategies, we will prevent, detect early, and effectively manage dysphagia, ensuring safe swallowing, optimal nutrition, and enhanced well-being for all. Mission : Our mission is to promote nutrition security for individuals with difficulty in chewing and swallowing, due to structural, neurological, and cognitive conditions, through research, development, training, and community empowerment. We aim to prevent malnutrition by advancing interdisciplinary research in Dietetics, Economics, Food Technology, Medicine, Nutrition, Occupational Therapy, Speech Language Pathology, and Surgery (arranged alphabetically). Our key goals are: Sharing Knowledge Across Disciplines : Bridging gaps between normal and disordered swallowing in relation to nutrition. 2. Providing a Multidisciplinary Forum : Facilitating knowledge sharing, knowledge production, and affordable, accessible, and cost-effective solutions for individuals across all income groups—low, middle, and high. 3. Enhancing Interdisciplinary Communication : Developing protocols for dysphagia care in alignment with the International Classification of Health Interventions (ICHI-WHO) . 4. Advocacy for Nutrition Security : Ensuring nutrition security for babies (3-6 years), children in schools, individuals with legally disabling conditions, and temporarily disadvantaged groups. We strive to unite nutrition experts and multidisciplinary professionals nationwide to advance dysphagia care through: Developing Evidence-Based Guidelines : Creating nutritional guidelines and protocols for swallowing rehabilitation. 2. Promoting Awareness and Training : Educating healthcare providers and communities to prevent and manage dysphagia-related malnutrition. 3. Fostering Research and Policy Advocacy : Ensuring equitable access to innovative nutritional therapies across diverse settings in India. We aim to lead national efforts in dysphagia awareness, research, and training by: Standardizing Protocols : Developing protocols for screening, assessment, and management of dysphagia. 2. Building Capacity : Offering multidisciplinary education and certification programs. 3. Advocating for Policy Integration : Integrating clinical nutrition and critical care policies. 4.
